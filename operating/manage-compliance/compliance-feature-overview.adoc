= Compliance feature overview
include::modules/common-attributes.adoc[]
:context: compliance-feature-overview

toc::[]

[role="_abstract"]

The compliance feature ensures that your OpenShift clusters adhere to industry standards and regulatory requirements. It provides automated compliance checks that you can use to continuously monitor your clusters against predefined OpenShift Compliance Operator benchmarks. These include Center for Internet Security (CIS), Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI-DSS), and Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program (FedRAMP) benchmarks. The feature includes detailed reports and remediation guidance to help administrators quickly identify and resolve compliance issues.

Additionally, {product-title-short} supports tailored profiles from Compliance Operator, where you can customize existing benchmarks. For example, you can change rules to match an organization's specific System and Organization Controls 2 (SOC 2) or the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) 800-171 requirements. You can schedule these compliance scans directly from the {product-title-short} console.

For non-OpenShift clusters, {product-title-short} previously provided default compliance profiles to scan user workloads. This functionality was removed and workload compliance for non-OpenShift clusters is provided through standard and custom security policies. You can use standard {product-title-short} security policies to replace most of the checks that were done by earlier compliance scans. You can tag specific security policies with relevant compliance benchmark controls. The {product-title-short} policy engine will continuously evaluate your workloads against these tagged policies and give you pass or fail output.
