= About GRPCRoute policy attachment

:FeatureName: `GRPCRoute` policy attachment

include::snippets/technology-preview.adoc[]

[role="_abstract"]
Beginning with {prodname} 1.4.0, `GRPCRoute` policy attachment is supported. You can now use `GRPCRoute` custom resources (CRs) for your Remote Procedure Call (gRPC)-native applications and services in all of your {prodname} policies except `TokenRateLimitPolicy` CRs. You can migrate existing policies after you create your `GRPCRoute` CRs.

{prodname} policies can now target `GRPCRoute` resources with the `targetRef` parameter. Specifically, `AuthPolicy`, `RateLimitPolicy`, and `PlanPolicy` accept `GRPCRoute` as a valid target `kind`. The following well-known attributes are also available for Common Expression Language (CEL) expressions:

* `request.grpc.service`: Represents the exact service being called.
* `request.grpc.method`: Represents the exact function being executed.

Using a `GRPCRoute` CR instead of an `HTTPRoute` CR can be an advantage in the following scenarios:

* Most of your traffic is internal or mobile-based, and not browser-based.
* You are working with real-time data or large datasets and need to push continuous updates to a client.
* You have microservice-to-microservice communication at a high rate per second. `GRPCRoute` policy attachment support is especially useful when combined with rate-limiting policies in these situations.
* You need long-lived connections.
* You want API changes to be contract-based, predictable, and visible.
* You want fine-grained security and rate-limiting to specific gRPC services and methods, but not the entire `Gateway` object.

The following CRs support `GRPCRoute` CRs in their `targetRef` fields:

** AuthPolicy CRs
** RateLimitPolicy CRs
** PlanPolicy CRs: Additional configuration might apply.

`DNSPolicy` and `TLSPolicy` policies cover attached `GRPCRoute` CRs automatically.

Telemetry works with `GRPCRoute` CRs with no updates required. While telemetry objects target the `Gateway` object, telemetry captures and reports on traffic flowing through attached `GRPCRoute` CRs. You have the same level of visibility for gRPC traffic as you currently do for HTTP and HTTPS traffic.
