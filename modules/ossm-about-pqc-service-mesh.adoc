= About {pqc-short} in service mesh

[role="_abstract"]

{pqc}, also known as quantum-resistant cryptography, uses encryption algorithms designed to resist attacks from quantum computers. 

Quantum computers use principles of quantum mechanics to perform certain calculations significantly faster than classical computers, compromising widely used cryptographic algorithms.

Most current encryption methods rely on mathematical problems that classical computers cannot solve in a practical time. Large-scale quantum computers could solve some of these problems more efficiently, which would weaken the security of existing cryptographic systems.

In {SMProductName}, cryptographic algorithms protect control plane and data plane communications, including mutual TLS (mTLS) between workloads. Enabling PQC strengthens these communications by introducing quantum-resistant key exchange mechanisms while maintaining compatibility with existing infrastructure.

[NOTE]
====
{pqc} algorithms are not available on {ocp-short-name} clusters running in FIPS mode.
====
