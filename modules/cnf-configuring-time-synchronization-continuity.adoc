= Configuring GNSS/NTP failover

Telco operators require time source redundancy to ensure time synchronization continuity and system stability for environments demanding time accuracy within 1.5 microseconds.

OpenShift Container Platform provides automatic failover capabilities to maintain synchronization. The system utilizes GNSS (delivered by phc2sys) as the Primary Time Source. To protect against primary signal loss, such as jamming or antenna failure, the system automatically transitions to the Secondary Time Source, NTP delivered by `chronyd``. Upon signal recovery, the system automatically switches back to and resumes synchronization with phc2sys.

Control the failover process by configuring the `ts2phc.holdover` parameter. The value, defined in seconds, dictates the maximum duration the system will rely on its internal clock before declaring GNSS signal loss. Failover to NTP initiates when the `ts2phc` process exits the configured holdover state. Configuring `ts2phc.holdover` directly controls the resilience of the system and the speed of the reaction to primary signal loss.
