= Shared infrastructure between hosted and standalone control planes

As a service provider, you can more effectively use your resources by sharing infrastructure between a standalone {product-title} control plane and {hcp}. A 3-node {product-title} cluster can be a management cluster for a hosted cluster. 

Sharing infrastructure can be beneficial in constrained environments, such as in the following situations:

* You have only a few large bare-metal machines, and you want to use them as efficiently as possible
* You want to implement more secure boundaries between your applications and your management plane

Before you share infrastructure, ensure that your infrastructure can support the load of {hcp}. On the {product-title} management cluster, nothing else can be deployed except {hcp}. Workload must not be demanding, and it must fall within a low queries-per-second (QPS) profile. To maintain a reasonable risk profile, you can share up to 3 hosted clusters.
