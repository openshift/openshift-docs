= About autonomous agentic troubleshooting

[role="_abstract"]
Autonomous agentic troubleshooting is an opt-in feature of {ols-long} that transforms incident response by allowing an AI agent to diagnose alerts and propose one-click fixes in the {ocp-product-title} web console within minutes of an alert firing.

The feature introduces KubeKlaw, an AI-powered agent that operates as an autopilot site reliability engineering (SRE) model for your cluster. KubeKlaw continuously monitors high-level policies for full lifecycle management, focusing on self-healing systems and reliability engineering.

KubeKlaw follows an alert-to-resolution pipeline:

. It receives Prometheus alerts through an Alertmanager webhook.
. It investigates using real cluster tools such as `oc` and `kubectl` to form a diagnosis.
. It proposes a diagnosis, risk assessment, and exact remediation actions.
. All critical steps require human approval in the web console. The AI proposes, Kubernetes enforces, and humans decide.
. Upon approval, the agent executes the remedy in a sandboxed pod with least-privilege role-based access control (RBAC).
