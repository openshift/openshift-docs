= Using {rhoai} with {microshift-short}

Using {rhoai} with {microshift-short} requires the following general workflow:

Getting your model ready::

* Choose the model that best aligns with your edge application and the decisions that need to be made at {microshift-short} deployment sites.
* In the cloud or datacenter, develop, train, and test your model.

Setting up the deployment environment::

* Configure your {op-system} and {microshift-short} for the specific hardware your deployment runs on, including driver and device plugins.

* To enable GPU/hardware accelerators for {microshift-short}, follow the device guidance on what you need to install. For troubleshooting, consult the device documentation or product support.
+
[TIP]
====
Using only a driver and device plugin instead of an Operator might be more resource efficient.
====
+
** For example, to use an NVIDIA GPU accelerator, you might begin by reading the following NVIDIA documentation:

*** link:https://docs.nvidia.com/datacenter/cloud-native/edge/latest/nvidia-gpu-with-device-edge.html#running-a-gpu-accelerated-workload-on-red-hat-device-edge[Running a GPU-Accelerated Workload on Red Hat Device Edge] (NVIDIA documentation)
*** link:https://docs.nvidia.com/datacenter/cloud-native/gpu-operator/latest/index.html[About the NVIDIA GPU Operator]
*** link:https://github.com/NVIDIA/k8s-device-plugin[k8s-device-plugin]
*** link:https://docs.nvidia.com/datacenter/cloud-native/container-toolkit/latest/install-guide.html[Installing the NVIDIA Container Toolkit]
*** link:https://docs.nvidia.com/datacenter/tesla/driver-installation-guide/index.html[Driver Installation Guide]
*** link:https://docs.nvidia.com/cuda/cuda-installation-guide-linux/[NVIDIA CUDA Installation Guide for Linux]

* Restart {microshift-short} if you are adding the RPM after starting {microshift-short}.
