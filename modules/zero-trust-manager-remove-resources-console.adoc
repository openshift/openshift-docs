= Removing {zero-trust-full} resources

Once you have uninstalled the {zero-trust-full}, you have the option to eliminate its associated resources from your cluster.

.Prerequisites

* You have access to the cluster with `cluster-admin` privileges.
* You have access to the {product-title} web console.

.Procedure

. Log in to the {product-title} web console.

. Remove the deployments of the zero trust workload identity manager components, such as `zero trust workload identity manager`, `cainjector`, and `webhook`, present in the `zero trust workload identity manager` namespace.

.. Click the *Project* drop-down menu to see a list of all available projects, and select the *zero trust workload identity manager* project.

.. Navigate to *Workloads* -> *Deployments*.

.. Select the deployment that you want to delete.

.. Click the *Actions* drop-down menu, and select *Delete Deployment* to see a confirmation dialog box.

.. Click *Delete* to delete the deployment.

.. Alternatively, delete deployments of the zero trust workload identity manager components such as `zero trust workload identity manager`, `cainjector` and `webhook` present in the `zero trust workload identity manager` namespace by using the command-line interface (CLI).
+
[source,terminal]
----
$ oc delete deployment -n zero trust workload identity manager -l app.kubernetes.io/instance=cert-manager
----

. Optional: Remove the custom resource definitions (CRDs) that were installed by the {zero-trust-full}:

.. Navigate to *Administration* -> *CustomResourceDefinitions*.

.. Enter `certmanager` in the *Name* field to filter the CRDs.

.. Click the Options menu {kebab} next to each of the following CRDs, and select *Delete Custom Resource Definition*:

*** `Certificate`
*** `CertificateRequest`
*** `CertManager` (`operator.openshift.io`)
*** `Challenge`
*** `ClusterIssuer`
*** `Issuer`
*** `Order`

. Optional: Remove the `cert-manager-operator` namespace.
.. Navigate to *Administration* -> *Namespaces*.
.. Click the Options menu {kebab} next to the *zero trust workload identity manager* and select *Delete Namespace*.
.. In the confirmation dialog, enter `zero trust workload identity manager` in the field and click *Delete*.
