= Creating the Istio and IstioCNI resources in RHEL 10 systems

For Red Hat Enterprise Linux 10 based systems and Red Hat Enterprise Linux CoreOS nodes, enable native nftables support in {SMProduct} 3.3 by configuring the `values.global.nativeNftables` field in the `{istio}` and `IstioCNI` resources.

.Prerequisties

* You have installed the {SMProductShortName} Operator.

* You are logged in to the {ocp-product-title} web console as cluster-admin.

.Procedure

. In the {ocp-product-title} web console, click *Operators* -> *Installed Operators*.

. Select `istio-cni` in the *Project* drop-down menu.

. Click *{SMProductShortName} Operator*.

. Click *IstioCNI* in the *Provided APIs* column.

. Click the `IstioCNI` instance, named `default`, in the *Name* column.

. Click *YAML* to view the `IstioCNI` configuration and make changes similar to the following example:
+
[source,yaml,subs="attributes,verbatim"]
----
apiVersion: sailoperator.io/v1
kind: IstioCNI
metadata:
  name: default
spec:
  namespace: istio-cni
  values:
    global:
      nativeNftables: true
# ...
----

. Click *Create* to deploy the {istio} CNI plugin.
+
To verify that the {istio} CNI plugin is successfully deployed, see if the *Status* column shows `State` as `Healthy`.

. In the {ocp-product-title} web console, click *Operators* -> *Installed Operators*.

. Select `istio-system` in the *Project* drop-down menu.

. Click *{SMProductShortName} Operator*.

. Click *{istio}* in the *Provided APIs* column.

. Click the `{istio}` instance, named `default`, in the *Name* column.

. Click *YAML* to view the `Istio` configuration and make changes similar to the following example:
+
[source,yaml,subs="attributes,verbatim"]
----
apiVersion: sailoperator.io/v1
kind: Istio
metadata:
  name: default
spec:
  namespace: istio-system
  values:
    global:
      nativeNftables: true
# ...
----

. Click *Create* to deploy the {istio} control plane.
+
To verify that the {istio} is successfully deployed, see if the *Status* column shows `State` as `Healthy`.
