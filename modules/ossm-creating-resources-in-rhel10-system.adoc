= Creating the Istio and IstioCNI resources in RHEL 10 systems

Nftables is a successor of iptables tool in the Linux kernel. The iptables interface has been deprecated and replaced by the nftables in Red Hat Enterprise Linux 10 (RHEL 10) systems. To deploy {istio} in a RHEL 10 system, you must set a feature flag as `values.global.nativeNftables=true` when you create two resources: `Istio` and `IstioCNI`. This feature was included in the {SMProduct} 3.2.

.Prerequisties

* The {SMProductShortName} Operator must be installed.

* You are logged in to the {ocp-product-title} web console as cluster-admin.

.Procedure

. In the {ocp-product-title} web console, click *Operators* -> *Installed Operators*.

. Select `istio-cni` in the *Project* drop-down menu.

. Click the {SMProductShortName} Operator.

. Click *IstioCNI* in the *Provided APIs* column.

. Click the `IstioCNI` instance, named `default`, in the *Name* column.

. Click *YAML* to view the `IstioCNI` configuration and make modifications:
+
.Example configuration
[source,yaml,subs="attributes,verbatim"]
----
apiVersion: sailoperator.io/v1
kind: IstioCNI
metadata:
  name: default
spec:
  namespace: istio-cni
  values:
    global:
      nativeNftables: true
# ...
----

. Click *Create*. This action deploys the Istio CNI plugin.
+
When `State: Healthy` appears in the *Status* column, the Istio CNI plugin is successfully deployed.

. In the {ocp-product-title} web console, click *Operators* -> *Installed Operators*.

. Select `istio-system` in the *Project* drop-down menu.

. Click the {SMProductShortName} Operator.

. Click *Istio* in the *Provided APIs* column.

. Click the `Istio` instance, named `default`, in the *Name* column.

. Click *YAML* to view the `Istio` configuration and make modifications:
+
.Example configuration
[source,yaml,subs="attributes,verbatim"]
----
apiVersion: sailoperator.io/v1
kind: Istio
metadata:
  name: default
spec:
  namespace: istio-system
  values:
    global:
      nativeNftables: true
# ...
----

. Click *Create*. This action deploys the {istio} control plane.
+
When `State: Healthy` appears in the *Status* column, {istio} is successfully deployed.
