= Etcd quorum restoration on the surviving node in two-node OpenShift cluster with fencing

[role="_abstract"]
During a standard node replacement, the system recovers automatically so you do not need to intervene manually. When a node fails and loses quorum, etcd stops on the surviving node to protect your data. You can monitor the Cluster Ecosystem Operator (CEO) conditions and Pacemaker status while you wait for etcd to stop. The CEO automatically detects the failed member and runs the quorum restore process. Pacemaker then brings etcd safely back online on the surviving node in single-member mode.
+
[NOTE]
====
If a temporary Redfish outage occurs during the fencing-job window, the CEO may remain in a degraded state. The expected behavior is that the cluster retries the fencing job after Redfish connectivity is restored. If the cluster does not recover automatically, manually clear the degraded condition or restart the CEO.
====
